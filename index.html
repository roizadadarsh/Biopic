Biography of Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs was born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California. He was the co-founder, chairman, and CEO of Apple Inc., and a major figure in the technology industry who played a pivotal role in the development of personal computers, smartphones, tablets, and digital media.

Jobs started Apple in 1976 along with Steve Wozniak in his parents' garage, and the company soon grew to revolutionize the technology industry with products like the Apple II, Macintosh, iPod, iPhone, and iPad. Known for his innovative vision, eye for design, and ability to anticipate market needs, Jobs transformed not only Apple but also the entire tech landscape.

Despite facing challenges, including being ousted from Apple in 1985, Jobs returned in 1997 and led the company to unprecedented success. His commitment to excellence, attention to detail, and passion for creating beautiful, functional products made him a beloved and iconic figure.

Steve Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011, but his legacy continues to inspire millions around the world. He remains a symbol of innovation, creativity, and the relentless pursuit of dreams.
